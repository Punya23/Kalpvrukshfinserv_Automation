### 1.1 Business Name Significance

**Kalpvruksh** — derived from Hindu mythology, the *Kalpavriksha* is the divine wish-fulfilling tree that grants all desires. This name reflects the company's philosophy: to be the single financial tree under which clients' every financial wish — protection, growth, security — is fulfilled.

### 1.2 Legal & Registration Background

Kalpvruksh Finserv operates as a financial distribution and advisory entity. The business activities span three major regulatory domains:

- **Insurance Distribution** — Governed by IRDAI (Insurance Regulatory and Development Authority of India). Sanjeev Surana holds a license as a direct insurance advisor and additionally operates as a Sales Manager under Star Health & Allied Insurance.
- **Mutual Fund Distribution** — Governed by SEBI and AMFI. The firm holds an ARN (AMFI Registration Number) to distribute mutual fund products from various AMCs (Asset Management Companies).
- **Loan Facilitation** — As a referral/DSA (Direct Sales Agent) partner for financial institutions like ICICI Bank for home loans and vehicle finance products.

**Need Analysis Framework for Life Insurance:**

```
Step 1: Income Assessment
        ↓
Step 2: Dependent Assessment (spouse, children, parents)
        ↓
Step 3: Liability Mapping (home loan, car loan, personal loans)
        ↓
Step 4: Existing Coverage Check
        ↓
Step 5: Human Life Value (HLV) Calculation
        [HLV = Annual Income × Years to Retirement]
        ↓
Step 6: Coverage Gap Analysis
        ↓
Step 7: Product Recommendation
        ↓
Step 8: Premium Affordability Check
        ↓
Step 9: Proposal & Underwriting
        ↓
Step 10: Policy Issuance & Onboarding
```

**Human Life Value (HLV) Formula:**

> Recommended Sum Assured = **Annual Income × 10–15 times** (minimum benchmark)
> Or: Outstanding Liabilities + Income Replacement for 15–20 Years

### 10.2 SOP: Policy Renewal Management

**This is the most critical revenue-protecting process for the business.**

```
D-60 Days Before Expiry:
  → Send First Reminder (WhatsApp + SMS)
  → Message: "Your policy expires on [DATE]. Let us discuss renewal options."

D-30 Days Before Expiry:
  → Call client personally
  → Discuss: Coverage adequacy, sum insured enhancement, add-ons
  → Generate renewal quote

D-15 Days Before Expiry:
  → Second reminder (WhatsApp + Call)
  → Send renewal payment link (if digital)
  → Address any queries or concerns

D-7 Days Before Expiry:
  → Urgent reminder
  → Emphasize: Lapse = loss of continuity benefit, waiting period reactivation

D-1 Day:
  → Final WhatsApp reminder
  → Offer to process payment immediately

Expiry Date:
  → Immediate follow-up if not renewed
  → Grace period: 30 days for health insurance
  → Escalate: Personal visit if needed

Post-Renewal:
  → Confirmation message sent
  → New policy document shared
  → Next renewal date updated in CRM
  → Coverage review check
```

**Renewal Dashboard Metrics to Track:**
- Policies due for renewal in next 30/60/90 days
- Renewal completion rate (target: 90%+)
- Lapsed policies (and reason)
- Premium grown YoY

### Appendix B: Useful Calculators & Formulas

**SIP Future Value:**
```
FV = P × [(1 + r/n)^(nt) - 1] / (r/n) × (1 + r/n)
P = Monthly SIP, r = annual rate, n = 12, t = years
```

**Human Life Value (HLV):**
```
HLV = Annual Income × (1 - Personal Expenses %) × Discount Rate Annuity Factor
Simplified: Annual Income × 10 to 15
```

**EMI Formula:**
```
EMI = P × r × (1+r)^n / [(1+r)^n - 1]
P = Principal, r = monthly rate, n = months
```

**Retirement Corpus:**
```
Monthly Expense × 12 × 25 (using 4% safe withdrawal rate)
Example: ₹50,000/month × 12 × 25 = ₹1.5 Crore corpus needed
```
